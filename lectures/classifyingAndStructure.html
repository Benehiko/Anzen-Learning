Where are we now?

Up to now, you have seen two videos. The second video described the FOUR ELEMENTS of information infrastructure. The introduction video has already described the first of these elements: Categorization. It briefly discussed categorization based on one or more of the following:

  • Functional
  • Alphabetical
  • Chronological
  • Geographical
  • Audience-based

We will now discuss the second element, organization.

What is organization?

Consider the following: A newspaper contains different sections, such as news, sport, arts and culture, and classifieds. The categorization of these groups of articles or elements has already been done. Now the editor of the newspaper should decide where these articles should be placed. For example, most important news will be first, followed by other news. Sport will be at the back and arts and culture will be added in a supplement. This task is organization, or provided structure.

How does that link to web sites?

Say a company has 250 pages for its web site. It will start by categorizing all those pages into categories. Only them can the designer decide where on the web site these different categories of pages will be displayed. The first is categorization, the latter is organization.

How do we do it?

The method of organization depends a lot on the nature of the information. The newspaper will be organized according to importance of articles or perhaps the behaviour of readers. Other information may be organized sequentially (as it comes). Hierarchies can also be used, e.g: About Us -- > Staff ---> Contact details. (Each staff member will have more than one piece of contact information. The company will have many staff members). Hyperlinks is also a great way of organizing information because it allows for easy navigation. Instead of adding one piece of information before or after another, this information can now be added within another piece of information.
