How do we do it?

Some of the numerous methods of involving users in the design of a site's information architecture, may include:

Card sorting: In stead of the web designers categorizing the information, this information can be provided to users. It can be written on cards of post-it notes and given to users to sort. This may be time-consuming because various users will have different categorization and organization schemes, but it will provide a lot of insight into the way users thinks. It also involves users in the beginning of the process.

Site prototyping: Create a "fake" or mock-up site - the layout of the site without any content. Users can then play around on the site with the designers present. Users can talk through their experience with the designers, highlighting issues.

Research methods such as surveys and focus groups. A survey is a written or electronic questionnaire asking users specific questions. Focus groups take more time and money, but provides a lot more insight. (A focus group consists of a group of 10 to 12 people answering questions and having discussions about a specific topic - in this case, the web site.)

Persona's: Create a typical user. (Yes, you can even give him or her a name). Now create a whole personality for this user, including his/her preferences and personality. Always ask the question: Will our typical user be able to use this site?
